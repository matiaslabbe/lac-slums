The LAC Slums Comprehensive Dataset

A harmonized, multi-country panel of informal settlements across Latin America and the Caribbean

Paul Gertler  ·  Marco González-Navarro  ·  Raimundo Undurraga  ·  Joaquín Urrego  ·  Matías Labbe

About

This dataset compiles NGO records and official government registries of informal settlements (slums, favelas, campamentos, asentamientos) across 11 Latin American countries, harmonized into a common schema and linked to city boundaries and household surveys. The goal is to track slum formation, growth, and formalization across multiple census waves.

This dataset serves as the empirical foundation for the companion working paper (see link above).

The project covers two analytical branches:

  • Slum-level panel — location, size, infrastructure access, and distance to urban amenities across waves.
  • Household-level repeated cross-section (Chile) — who lives in informal settlements, entry and exit dynamics, and resident selection.

Countries covered

Argentina Brazil Chile Colombia Costa Rica Guatemala Honduras Mexico Perú Paraguay Uruguay
View sample map (PDF)

Key variables

For each slum unit: country, census year, settlement name, municipality, household count, access to electricity, piped water, sewerage, land title, and polygon geometry. Slum locations are linked to the nearest urban boundary and classified as inside city, fringe (<50 km buffer), or remote.

Chilean Slum Cross-Section Cohort Data

Household-level data from three waves of the Catastro Nacional de Campamentos, Ministerio de Vivienda y Urbanismo (MINVU), Chile. All waves include year of arrival. The 2019 and 2022 waves include a comprehensive survey with individual-level variables (education, housing quality, aspirations).
