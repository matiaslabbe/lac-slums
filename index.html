The LAC Slums Comprehensive Dataset

A harmonized, multi-country panel of informal settlements across Latin America and the Caribbean

Paul Gertler  ·  Marco González-Navarro  ·  Raimundo Undurraga  ·  Joaquín Urrego  ·  Matías Labbe

Working Paper — Data under active assembly

About

This dataset compiles official government registries of informal settlements (slums, favelas, campamentos, asentamientos) across 11 Latin American countries, harmonized into a common schema and linked to city boundaries and household surveys. The goal is to track slum formation, growth, and formalization across multiple census waves.

The project covers two analytical branches:

  • Slum-level panel — location, size, infrastructure access, and distance to urban amenities across waves.
  • Household-level panel (Chile) — who lives in informal settlements, entry and exit dynamics, and resident selection.

Countries covered

Argentina Brazil Chile Colombia Costa Rica Guatemala Honduras Mexico Nicaragua Paraguay Uruguay

Key variables

For each slum unit: country, census year, settlement name, municipality, household count, access to electricity, piped water, sewerage, land title, and polygon geometry. Slum locations are linked to the nearest urban boundary and classified as inside city, fringe (<50 km buffer), or remote.

Harmonized panel files

panel_latam.csv — Harmonized attribute panel
All slum observations, all countries and waves. Common schema: country, year, settlement name, municipality, household count, infrastructure access indicators.
Format: CSV  |  Status: Under assembly  |  Download: coming soon
panel_latam.gpkg — Spatial panel (GeoPackage)
Same as CSV plus polygon/point geometry. Mixed geometry (polygon where available, centroid otherwise).
Format: GeoPackage  |  Status: Under assembly  |  Download: coming soon
Chile household panel — Catastro de Campamentos 2011–2022
Individual-level merged survey across three waves. Variables: education, gender, income, housing quality, year of arrival, aspirations.
Format: Stata (.dta)  |  Status: Restricted  |  Access: contact authors
